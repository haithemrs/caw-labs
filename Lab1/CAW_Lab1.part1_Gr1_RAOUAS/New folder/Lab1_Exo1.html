Brief History

The State of Palestine has a long and complex history rooted in ancient civilizations. For centuries, it has been a crossroads of cultures and religions, playing a vital role in the development of the Middle East.

In the early 20th century, after the fall of the Ottoman Empire, the region came under British control and later witnessed the establishment of Israel in 1948 a turning point that displaced hundreds of thousands of Palestinians, leading to decades of conflict and occupation.

Despite ongoing challenges, the Palestinian people continue to struggle for self-determination and independence. Today, the Palestinian Authority governs parts of the West Bank, while Gaza remains under siege, enduring severe humanitarian crises.
